New to selling gift cards? Start here

A gift card (or "voucher" / "e-code") is prepaid money loaded onto a specific store - like Amazon, iTunes or Steam. The problem in Cameroon is simple: many of these cards only work for shoppers inside the USA, UK or Canada, or only buy things you don't actually need. So a card someone abroad sent you can feel like "money you can't touch."

That's what we fix. We buy that card from you and pay you real, spendable cash (XAF) into your mobile money - so the gift finally becomes money you can use for anything: data, fees, business, savings.

E-code vs physical card

An e-code is a digital code sent to your email or phone (a string of letters and numbers). A physical card is the plastic card with a code on the back, usually under a scratch panel. We buy both - for physical cards you just send a clear photo of the code.

What affects the rate you get

Gift card rates change daily and depend on three things: the card brand (Amazon, Apple, Steam etc. trade at different rates), the country the card is from (a US card and a UK card of the same value pay differently), and whether it's an e-code or physical card. That's why we always quote you live before you send anything - so you know exactly what you'll receive.

How to stay safe
